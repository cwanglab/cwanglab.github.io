<p class="eyebrow">Openings</p>

We are recruiting **PhD students, a postdoc, and visiting scholars** to work on heterogeneous medical AI — population-scale imaging, federated and privacy-preserving learning, and lightweight on-device inference. Most projects sit somewhere on our **Cloud–Edge–Terminal** framework; before applying, read the [research directions](/research/) and pick the one that fits you.

The PhD and postdoc roles are research positions; visiting scholars (PhD students, postdocs, or faculty on sabbatical) join for fixed periods on an agreed project. If you are unsure which role or direction fits, say so in your email and we will advise.

<p class="eyebrow">Fit</p>

## Who we're looking for

We read every application properly, so we would rather be specific than encouraging.

You will fit well if you have solid programming (Python, PyTorch) and a real interest in one of the directions above — not a general wish to "do AI in healthcare". Cloud projects assume comfort with large datasets and statistics; Edge and Terminal projects assume some systems, optimisation, or efficiency background. Clinical curiosity matters: much of our work runs against NHS-governed data and real intervention workflows, so reproducibility, careful evaluation, and data governance are part of the job, not an afterthought.

You do not need prior publications, and you do not need to already know our methods. You do need to have looked at what we actually do — a message that could have been sent to any lab will not get a useful reply.

<p class="eyebrow">Apply</p>

## How to apply

Email **[Chengjia.Wang@hw.ac.uk](mailto:Chengjia.Wang@hw.ac.uk)** with three things:

- a **CV**;
- a **transcript** (undergraduate, and master's if you have one);
- a **one-page statement** covering which Cloud / Edge / Terminal direction you want to work on, why it interests you, and your career goal.

Funded PhD routes are available through Heriot-Watt University and Centre for Doctoral Training (CDT) scholarships; these are competitive and their eligibility and deadlines vary by scheme, so email first and we will point you to the route that fits your situation. Postdoc and visiting-scholar arrangements depend on current funding and timing — ask, and we will tell you honestly what is open.

You can read more about the group on the [team](/team/) and [publications](/publications/) pages, and about the PI and lab at [hintelligence.online](https://hintelligence.online).

## Questions

**Is the position funded?**
Some PhD places are funded via Heriot-Watt / CDT competitive scholarships; postdoc posts are funded when a grant is active; visiting scholars normally come with their own sponsorship or scholarship. Funding status changes over time — email to ask what is currently open before assuming.

**Do I need a master's degree?**
It helps, but a strong bachelor's with research experience is considered. Under the UK system, applicants without a master's may enter through an MRes or integrated route depending on background.

**Can international applicants apply?**
Yes. Eligibility and funding cover differ by scheme — some scholarships are open to international students, others are restricted — so we will check the specifics for your case when you write.

**What will I actually work on?**
A concrete problem on one of the Cloud, Edge, or Terminal directions, shaped around your strengths and our current clinical and data partnerships rather than a fixed, pre-written plan. We co-define the project in the first months.

**How long until I hear back?**
We aim to reply to genuine, specific applications. A targeted email that shows you have read the research directions is far more likely to get a substantive response than a generic one.
